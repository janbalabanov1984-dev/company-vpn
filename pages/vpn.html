3 Steps to Privacy

1. Encapsulation

Your data is wrapped in a secure packet on your device before it ever leaves your network interface.

2. The Tunnel

Traffic travels through a "tunnel" to our xVPN server. To ISPs and hackers, this just looks like random noise.

3. Decryption & Exit

To the outside world (your ISP, hackers on Wi-Fi), it looks like gibberish. They can see that you are connected to xVPN, but they cannot see what you are doing. Our server decrypts your request and sends it to the internet. The website sees our IP, not yours.

Your Threat Model

What we protect you against.

Public Wi-Fi

Coffee shops and airports are hunting grounds for packet sniffers. xVPN blocks them entirely.

ISP Tracking

Your Internet Service Provider can legally sell your browsing history. We blind them.

Geo-Blocking

Access content restricted to specific regions by virtually "moving" your location.

Post-Quantum Readiness

Classic encryption (RSA/ECC) may be broken by powerful quantum computers in the next decade. This threat is known as "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later".

When you connect to xVPN, your device initiates a handshake with our server, establishing a secure tunnel. xVPN is designed with emerging cryptographic threats in mind. We are testing NIST-standardized Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) algorithms to ensure your data remains safe long into the future.
